# Product Context

## Why this site exists
The portfolio is the central public surface for BytesByBlinken. It needs to:

- Convince a hiring manager skimming for 30 seconds that Chris is a credible
  developer with shipped work.
- Give a small-business or local client enough confidence to start a
  conversation about a website or web app build.
- Give blog readers a clean reading experience and a way to find older posts.

## Audience expectations
- Recruiters expect: clear role/identity, summary of skills, evidence of shipped
  projects, easy way to reach out.
- Clients expect: visible past work (StressFree Site is the strongest signal),
  professional tone, working contact path.
- Blog readers expect: legible long-form text, dated posts, archive controls,
  ability to leave comments.

## User flows preserved by the refresh
- Visitor lands on homepage and scrolls hero -> projects -> skills -> blog
  preview -> contact/footer.
- Visitor clicks Blog -> lands on `blog.html` -> can filter by month, read
  posts, and leave comments.
- Admin (Chris) logs in on `blog.html` -> can create/edit/delete posts and
  moderate comments. This functionality must keep working untouched by the
  visual refresh.

## Tone
Professional and editorial. Personal warmth comes from the writing and project
context, not from heavy gradient graphics or playful chrome. Dark mode remains
supported and should feel just as intentional as light mode.
